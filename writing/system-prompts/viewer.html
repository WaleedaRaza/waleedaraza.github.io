80 years in 15 minutes.

There's a story about a painter who, at the end of his life, could complete a masterpiece in 15 minutes. Someone asked how. He said: "It took me 80 years to learn how to do it in 15 minutes."

These prompts are that. ~1.5 years of editing. Input from devs across FAANG, startups, solo builders. Accumulated failures, breakthroughs, and hard-won patterns. Every sentence has been questioned, tested, refined. What you're reading is compressed experience—not theory.

The difference is the words you know how to say.

Prompt engineering isn't magic. It's accumulated knowledge externalized. The gap between someone who struggles for 8 months and someone who solves it in a week isn't intelligence—it's knowing what to ask. The right framing. The right sequence. The right constraints.

My approach: peel back the onion. Every conversation with an LLM is layers—surface request, underlying assumption, hidden context, actual goal. Most people prompt at the surface. The leverage is deeper. These frameworks teach you to think in layers before you type.
