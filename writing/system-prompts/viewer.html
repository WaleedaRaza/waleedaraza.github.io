THE PROBLEM

If you've vibe coded, you know.

LLM coding tools like Cursor, Claude Code, and Copilot are excellent for practice and non-distributed projects. But when your goals change—when you're building something real—without a holistic plan, you hit the wall:

  • Context collapse across sessions
  • Hallucination-driven debug loops
  • Fixes that break three other things
  • Plans that sound great but never get written
  • Code you can ship but can't explain
THE SOLUTION

Partly an LLM problem we can't solve. Partly a prompting problem we can.

We can't directly tune the LLMs these tools use—but we can impact the prompting we do, and the rules/system prompts we give our tools. These 5 frameworks address the human side of the equation.

BUILT ON EXPERIENCE

Tested by dozens of devs across FAANG and startups.

These prompts are only as effective as the experiences they're built on. Devs who've added these to their Cursor rules section report more efficiency and clearer thinking. But they're not finished—they're living documents.

Edit them. Adapt them to your needs. Use GPT to customize them for your specific workflow. If you make improvements, reach out—I'll update the content here for everyone.
